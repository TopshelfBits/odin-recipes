Ingredients

  • Double pie crust (recipe)
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 1/4 lbs Granny Smith apples (peeled, cored)
  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 3 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg

Steps

  1. Make the pie crust of your choice. (recipe) Preheat oven to 425˚F.
  2. Melt butter in a saucepan over medium. Whisk 3 Tbsp flour then simmer for 1 minute. Whisk constantly, adding in 1/4 cup water, 1 cup sugar and boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 3 minutes.
  3. Peel and remove cores. Thinly slice ~7 cups of apples. Place apples into a bowl and sprinkle the top with 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon. Toss to combine, and pour the sauce from Step 2. Stir to coat the apples well.
  4. Sprinkle where you will roll out bottom pie crust with flour. Make the pie crust to a 12" diameter. Wrap around a rolling pin to transfer to a 9" pie plate. Add the mixture, with most of it in the center. (Careful not to fill the edges - which would make it difficult to seal)
  5. Bake at 425˚F in the center of oven for 15 minutes. Reduce the heat to 350˚F and bake for another 45 minutes or until the apples soften. Rest for 1 hour at room temp before enjoying.
